Pricing explained

The pricing plans offerend by the WHURTHAY web analytics platforms, comes in three categories *

  1. Chicko
  2. Breath
  3. Godum

Pricing/Plans page

* For enterprise pricing, please contact sales directly at sales@whurthay.com

Pricing explained:

For instance, consider the Breath plan. It has a fee of $51, every month. for access to analytics and tracking of 10000 unique visitors.

A lot of people, ask us, if the visitor tracking & analytics would stop after 10000 unique visiors have been tracked for that month, and the answer is NO.

Actually, what really happens is that only the latest 10000 users are visible and analysed each month, and this would be true, when you are on the Breath plan. As, the visitor number grows more than 10,000 users. The latest 10000 are kept, and the older which were the visitors at the end of that list of 10000 are hidden from the analysis and the dashboard list, once that happens.

Saying that, one can still request to get history of all the visitors to their website, which could be beyond their plan’s limit. This could be important for auditing or to identify someone, who bought from you eons ago.

To request any visitor details to your site, beyond your plan limit. Use the contact form under the dashboard account settings, or reach us directly at archived@whurthay.com

Product Overview

What’s this Whurthay thing?

Whurthay web analytics allows its users to tag & identify their website visitors.

The purpose is to identify & predict user behaviour on their website, and estimate traction.

The tagging feature allows you to identify your most frequent user & potential customers. It also allows
to identify which user segment, a visiting user belong to, and customize site/customer support/product
behaviour accordingly.

Accessing the platform

Tracking your website visitors using the Whurthay web analytics platform starts with the following easy to follow
steps:

Start registration

Get started by either clicking the register button on the landing page or entering your website address, and clicking the Track my site visitors button

Register

  • On the registraion page, Fill out all the details, and submit using the register button

  • Registraion page is a bit different, if you’ve already entered the website address at the home page.
    If the registraion page is something like the one shown above, Fill in the remaining details, and submit using the
    Register button

  • You would be requested to confirm your email address.

  • You would be shown this page, once you click the ‘confirm email’ button, in the confirmation email

Once you re-login, you have to choose a plan, or go with the default selected Breath plan, Enter
your payment details, once that’s done, you gain access to the platform.

Once your payment is successful, you are shown instructions on how to start tracking your website visitors,
using the unique code, generated for your particular website.

Once the tracking code is installed properly, the tracking data gets collected and displayed on your site
dashboard

The Dashboard

The Dashboard would be the entrance to your whurthay web analytics platform, after signing in.

It is customized for each user, based on their plan and the site it tracks.

The dashboard, shows minute details of every visitor to your site. The time/day of visit. If they are a
returning visitor, Which part of the world, do they belong to, Their browser preferences, If they are browsing
on a mobile, tablet or from a pc, their browser and system brands, Operating system etc…

Once this data is aggregated, you get access to the analytics section which could be accessed using the
☰ hamburger icon, in the top left corner of the dashboard.

Analytics

Don’t estimate dips and highs in your web traffic anymore. As, analytics at Whurthay allows you to, access detailed view of your current & predicted web traffic trends.
Highs & lows in visitor trends is depicted, analysed & predicted, based on visitors frequency of your website.

Usage by device: if the user is accessing your website, using a dektop, mobile or a tablet is segmented as pies, for the user to access and analyse

Usage by Browser: The browser used by the website users, is grouped, stored and mapped as pies, for the website users to access and analyse

The countries where your webapp is most and least popular, gives you a detailed insight into, your target market, and the cultures and people that are more focused and excited to use your product, shows areas you can put more focus into, and give insights into markets, that still needs to be captured. This would help make businesses more diverse in the long run.

A world map is used to highlight countries and parts of the world, where your application is the most popular. Gives insights into, people and cultures, which find your application, the most appealing.

Operating system popularity, among your website users, gives understanding your user choices, and speding habits. As according to statistics, mac users are said to prefer quality, and are willing to pay a higher price, for better user experience. Thus, if your user’s are mostly using a mac, you know, you need to focus more on quality and user experience, and in turn are allowed to, and can A/B test to charge a higher price, for a higher quality product.

A sun burst chart is also generated for easily visualizing and accessing operating system usage and preferences by your website users.

The moment you assign different tags to your visitors, using the dashboard add a tag feature, It also generates a tag group scatter plot, under the analytics section, grouping users based on the tag, they are assigned on the dashboard. This gives an in-depth view of the group of visitors that belong to different segments, based on their usage and frequency habits of your website product.

Users are also segmented as new, returning or old customers. This allows us to comprehend user interest, as more returning customer, means higher interest group user, which in turn means, better content or landing page of the tracked website. But, higher numbers could eventually be targeted, based on these numbers. This would result in imporving one’s website, for higher returning and new visitors/potential/customers to website

Explaining visitor data

Visitors are segemented based on, if it a spider bot that is visiting, or a realy actual human.
Other segments are based on device/brand info, machine info, browser usage statistics, and if a user is new, old or returning visitor.
